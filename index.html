Make Bootable SD Card for OpenBoard Pegasus V4

May 11th, 2014 | Comments

Openboard - Pegasus PCB has been released for 2 months. I am working hard to boot it up with Niuboot and Linux. Now it is well done and I am glad to show you the steps here how to make a SD card to boot your openboard.

Step 1: Prepare Your Hardware

  1. A Micro-SD card (formal name TF card) less than 2G if your board is mounted with MX233CAG4B. Or any capacity of card if MX233CAG4C.

  2. A SD card reader normally comes with a USB port. Or a SD card converter suit (micro to normal size) if your notebook computer has a SD/MMC card socket.

For further debug, you also need following devices.

  1. A USB-Serial converter wire.

Note that openboard only support CMOS (3.3V) signal with its Debug UART port. FYR, I use a cp1202 as the converter.

  1. And of course, an openboard of h/w ver. pegasus.

Step 2: Make partition on your sd card

  • Insert your sd card into your desktop computer or notebook.

Normally you will find some new device node appears in your /dev directory. They have different name base on your sd reader types, such as /dev/sdb, /dev/mmc0p1.

  1. Switch to root or use sudo in debian/ubuntu.

  2. Run fdisk

sudo fdisk /dev/sdb

  1. Delete all old partitions. CAUTION All data on your sd card will lost.
